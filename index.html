Motivation

Why we chose this topic and what user problem we aim to address.

Write here why your group selected this project idea. Explain the real-world issue, who is affected, and why this problem is worth solving.

You can also mention the initial design opportunity, the user pain points, and the value your solution intends to create.

Research and Brainstorming

Evidence, notes, early exploration, and initial idea development.

Summarise your research process here. You can discuss interviews, observations, questionnaires, competitor analysis, brainstorming notes, and any design references.

User Journey

Target users, pain points, goals, and step-by-step experience.

Explain the user journey of your main persona. Describe what the user wants to do, where they experience friction, and how your design improves the journey.

Wireframes / Figma

Low-fidelity ideas, layout exploration, and interface structure.

Add your early sketches, low-fidelity wireframes, and Figma screenshots here. You can also explain why you arranged the content in this way.

Design Iterations

How the design changed over time and why those changes were made.

Describe what feedback you received, what problems you noticed, and how you revised the design after each round of iteration.

Evaluation

Testing, feedback, findings, and design decisions based on evidence.

Summarise your evaluation results here. Mention what methods you used, what users said, what worked well, and what still needs improvement.

Final Reflection

What we learned, what worked, and what could be improved in future.

Write your final reflection here. You can discuss the overall strengths of your design, the limitations of your process, and how you would improve the project if you had more time.

You may also reflect on teamwork, communication, role distribution, and how your understanding of user-centred design changed during the project.

Technical Reflection on AI Use

Prompts, verification process, and critical reflection on how AI was used.

If your coursework requires AI reflection, document which AI tools were used, what prompts were given, how outputs were verified, and what limitations or risks were identified in the process.
